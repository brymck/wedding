Our Story

Maiko and I met at the UNC Chapel Hill in August 2005. I had just come back from a one-year study abroad program in Japan, and Maiko had just arrived as a study abroad student from her university in Japan. We met at a pig-picking party welcoming international students. Although it was not love at first sight—we couldn't remember each other's names the next time we met—we drew closer as we learned more about each other. We began dating a month later and were inseparable throughout that year.

After her study abroad program ended in May 2006, we completed our bachelors degrees in our respective universities, promising each other to move to California upon our graduation. I moved out to California first and found a job with Wachovia (now Wells Fargo) in Oakland; Maiko entered a masters program at the Monterey Institute of International Studies in Monterey to pursue a career as an interpreter. I drove two hours to see her every weekend, braving the early morning traffic on US-101 each Monday.

After two years, she graduated moved back to Japan to work with Honda as an in-house interpreter. I was awarded a scholarship by the Japanese government to attend Tokyo University as a research student. Again, we commuted to see each other; this time the two hours was by train.

Finally, after burnishing her credentials at Honda and toward the completion of my program, Maiko went freelance, we got engaged, and we moved into an apartment together. I've been working with BlackRock since summer 2012, and we've been enjoying our time together (finally!) as a couple since then. Our wedding ceremony in Japan was on October 8th, 2012, and we're looking forward to our ceremony in the US this coming April 27th!
